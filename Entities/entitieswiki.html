Entities

Description:

Numerous entities exist in VOTV, surprisingly, a very small amount of these entities can be spawned manually, while most either spawn naturally, randomly, or have to be forced spawned. Here, there will be three categories: Normal, Anomaly, and Forbidden. Normal, being entities that you come across regularly or aren't very special at all. Anomaly being entities that are somewhat or pretty rare, are special, or very mysterious. Forbidden being extremely rare, sometimes innaccessible, or super special. Some of them have very distinct stories that play into the overall lore of VOTV.

Normal Entities

Antibreather Passive Spawns near Romeo, in Antibreather Cave at night, then eventually despawns in the morning time. Antibreather (AB for short) is a quadrupedal entity that comes from a cave near the Romeo satellite. A Rozital-based entity, resembling a human body with stitches all around his body and metal legs (of which front pair look like a scythe blades). It comes every night (sometimes even on day) from its cave, roaming aimlessly around and illuminating it's path with a glowing white eyes.
Ariral Neutral Can be triggered by either the Ariral Stalker Event, Command, if the player steals an Ariral item, if the player touches an Ariral chest, if the player microwaves or woodchips an Argemia Plush, and Ariral Vent Break-in Event. The Ariral in VOTV has two variants, one variant being a stalker where it moves when the player moves but stops when the player stops. The stalker variant will still attack the player but in a more subtle way. The other variant is aggressive where the Ariral will hunt down the player, pick up and throw items, and attack the player. The Ariral entity usually and only spawns the Ariral Treehouse Landmark. 
The Ariral entity is representative of the Ariral race created by MrDrNose which is its own entire civilization. 
Three visit the the area that the player plays in, one short and two talls with the short presumably having escaped the shorts totalitarian division at some point. 
The two talls are the ones who mostly prank the player with slaps, item throws, and other events, while the short simply stays out of that business. 
But later the short eventually dies to one of the killer wisps after days 35-40, this is due to the cheap Indis suits that all three of them had on during that time. 
Some messages that are in the form of signals that the player can sometimes find are actually aimed towards the Arirals.
The Arirals come and visit Earth for a specific mission, now as to what that mission is is currently unknown.
The Arirals play a pretty large part to most of the events that appear in Storymode. 
More lore and info on the Arirals can be explored here: Ariral Lore Drive.
Furfur Passive Can be triggered by any toilet use at exactly 3:33 am, anywhere in the world, or anywhere in the untitled_83. Furfur is bipedal entity with a deer skull serving as his head, appearing only when the player uses the toilet at exactly 3:33 am, randomly spawning in the backrooms, or when the player takes a picture of it in a random location in the map. When it comes in contact with the player, it glows red for 2 seconds, drops 2 or more bones, and then disappears shortly after. One way of increasing it's spawn frequency is finding it's skull either in the Wooded Shack Landmark, digging it from a Shroom Circle, or finding it in a random dig spot, and then from there heating or burning that skull.
Hanged Man Passive Located under a bridge near Oscar Satellite Dish and the Lake. A shadow of a hanged man. Stationary, never moves. To ensure you seem him, you must enable shadow to 3 in render settings.
Fossilhound Aggressive Random spawn. Fossilhound is an extremely aggressive dog-like creature, appearing at nights behind the fence. It will start out in a hole and eventually climb out. Charging at the player as soon as it sees them, it can knock you down multiple times even through the fence. It will continue to chase you until it has hit you. If the player attacks back, the Fossilhound will break apart into a bunch of bones and pieces.
The Fossilhound actually originates from another yet older game that MrDrNose has made in the past, UDAG.
Insomnia Demon Aggressive Once triggered it usually spawns in the dark corners near the player. A tall transparent figure with big white eyes that appears after players sleep goes lower than 10%. It will start chasing down the player if sleep goes lower than 0.5%, sending you into a random dream.
Wolfgang Passive Can spawn randomly or be manually spawned by placing a pumpkin in the AB Cave. Wolfgang is a giant centipede that isn't harmful. He's very fast and can open the bases doors, he can also be ridden. He is attracted to pumpkins so placing any in any part of the world will cause him to move towards it.
Rufus Aggressive Spawned only by a Console Command. Rufus is a red 4 legged creature that has a long neck that is very flexible. It appears to have a face with two eyes and no other distinct features. Creepy music will play no matter where you are at in the world. When Rufus is near you a giant subtitle tells you to hide. Rufus will begin chasing you until it either catches you or you quickly enter in the Console Command. Once it catches you, it used to trigger the Secret Rufus Ending, but instead now it simply annihilates you. Rufus can be killed used the Arir Gun and explosions of any size. Once it dies it can drop a Thiccfus Plush. While dead it will just ragdoll.
Thiccfus Aggressive Spawned only by a Console Command. Thiccfus is a bipedal entity that is extremely fat, it has two large legs and two small arms. It's head barely sticks out of it's body. It appears to have a face with two eyes and no other distinct features. Creepy music will play no matter where you are at in the world. When Thiccfus encounters an obstacle like a prop or item, it will explode. When Thiccfus is near you a giant subtitle tells you to hide. Thiccfus will begin chasing you until it either catches you or you quickly enter in the Console Command. Once it catches you, it will annihilate you. Thiccfus can be killed used the Arir Gun and explosions of any size. While dead it will just ragdoll.
Fridge Aggressive Outside the barrier. Fridge is a black shadow-like figure. If a player illegally gets out or noclips out of the barrier Fridge will chase down the player until it crashes the player's game. It is a form of anticheat to stop the player from exploring further out of the bounds.
Walking Tree Passive Random spawn. A Walking Tree entity is a tree that moves. It moves when the player is not looking. However, looking through a camera does not count and you can see the tree move. Once it stops moving the entity transforms into a fully meshed out tree prop which may appear as a real tree when its not.
Live Erie Passive Grab Erie Plush Object and feed it. A Live Erie is an animated version of the Erie Plush Object, instead of crafting an Erie Kerfur Omega this Erie will move on its own and dance a little, it can pick up things and throw them. It also can be fed more to increase Erie Rep but if picked up and dropped, its Erie Rep will go down. This entity makes little noises when moving around. This entity while not hostile to the player or any other entities, will be quite destructive since it can make a mess out of your base. So be careful where you leave it. This is yet another reference/depiction of Erie character from AEWVS.
Cockroach Passive Triggered by any food that is left open. A small bug that searches for any food out in the open, it will eat it and grow, overtime the food will be eaten hence making the cockroach a lot larger. It can be killed by either stepping over it or using the swatter prop.
Wasps Neutral Dig up a waspnest, shake it, or damage it in any way. A small bug that cannot be damaged or interacted, it comes in swarms and will continously attack the player. The best way to mitigate this is by wearing a hazmat suit. To end it you must destroy the wasp nest completely.
Drone Passive Either at Main Base or Drone starting point. A flying drone that delivers items and objects from Main Base to the researchers in the game.
Fireflies Passive Random spawn, at night. A swarm of bright yellow bugs that can illuminate the darkness for you.
Fish Passive Use a fishing rod. It can come in many different variations, sizes, shapes, and colors. Just flops around, is unkillable, and can be obtained using a fishing rod.
Kavotia Aggressive Can be spawned manually through sandbox props or can spawn naturally around The Hole structure during the Rozital Hole Event. A Rozital-based entity that is hostile to the player, it has 3 legs, and a turret for a head. It can sit and move around. If it spots the player, it will begin firing purple beams which deal damage to the player eventually killing the player.

Anomaly Entities

Fire Wisp Neutral Random spawn, spawns once in untitled_133. A floating blue star-like entity that roams around the world and doesn't interact with the player. However, if the player were to step in its way or touch the entity the player would start burning up quickly causing the player's demise.
Red Wisp Aggressive Random spawn. A floating red star-like entity that quickly flashes between locations, it moves incredibily fast and is almost impossible to escape. It is very aggressive around the player, it will even follow and chase down the player from a map away. If this entity comes across any obstacles, objects, or entities it will explode, this is similar to Thiccfus' ability to explode.
Green Wisp Passive Can spawn randomly at night. The Green Wisp is another wisp variant but green. When it travels around, it will leave a little trail of green particles behind. The entity can chase the player but most of the times it usually just wanders around randomly. If the player were to interact with this entity, the player will be teleported randomly to any location on the map, ragdolled.
Pink Wisp Passive Spawns anywhere during Pink Wisps Event. A floating pink orb that's wandering around. Disappears as soon as player come closer to it.
White Wisp Hostile Random spawn. White wisp is a another wisp variant except completely white. The White Wisp will chase after or follow the player, it moves around randomly. If the player encounters or interacts with this entity, it will warp and distort the players screen so much that eventually it causes the game to crash. There is also a scream that can be heard during this encounter.
Black Wisp Hostile Random spawn. Black wisp is a another wisp variant except completely black. It has a warping effect around it in the shape of a sphere. The Black Wisp will not chase after or follow the player, it moves around randomly. If the player encounters or interacts with this entity, it will make a warp sound and completely blank out the player's screen, rendering the game broken. To fix this you must alt+f4 to exit.
Orange Wisp Passive Random spawn. Orange wisp is a another wisp variant except completely orange and a little smaller. This wisp can chase and follow the player sometimes, when touched the player will teleport to a random location and the player's game will be set to mirrored. This can be fixed in the Video Settings.
Purple Wisp Passive Random spawn. Purple Wisp is a another wisp variant except completely purple and less intact. It will leave a little trail of its essence as it moves. When the entity touches the player, it will cause time to start moving backward and it will do this forever until the player has restarted their game.
Killer Wisp Aggressive Random spawn, after Obelisk Event. A floating bright yellow star-like entity that strolls randomly in the world. Once it spots the player it will chase down the player, if the player is captured by this entity, the entity will rip apart the player causing the player's demise. This entity usually spawns at night and will disappear if you aren't looking at it while it is day. The Killer Wisp can also be found ripping apart a the undead deer corpse and usually that is it's first target.
Shadow Man Passive Random spawnA black shadow-like figure that moves incredibly fast around the player, in the newer versions instead of moving the Shadow Man teleports in quick intervals around the player, while in older versions he had to run around the player. To spot him a little easier, you can set your fps to a very low number and have an EMF reader out since he emits an EMF signal, you'll be able to catch glimpses of him as you walk around.
Piramid Passive Triggered by the Piramid Event. An updated version of the Tripod entity except with more functionality and an updated look. The Piramid entity, another Rozital-based entity, walks around and hunts any Killer Wisp that may be wandering around, once found the Piramid will suck up the Killer Wisp and once all Killer Wisps are hunted down the Piramid entity will simply leave and walk out. The Piramid is sent to "deal" with the Killer Wisp entities that was outlined as a warning during the Obelisk Event. The Piramid doesn't actually care about the player but instead serves protection for the Arirals.
Jellyfish Passive Spawns during the Jellyfish Event. The entity is seen as a purple flying jellyfish. Which once spawned flies around in a random direction until they despawn after a long while.
Deer Neutral Random spawn. An animal that spawns very often, the Deer can be seen strolling around the forest, however when you look at it or spot it the Deer will slowly fade out of existence. The Deer moves at a walking pace. If you were to teleport near it you'd be attacked and pushed back.
Undead Deer Aggressive Spawns during the Undead Deer Event. After obtaining the Deer Signal the regular Deer Entity will stop spawning and instead be replaced with an undead version of itself, along with it's edible corpse that the player can find and eat. It moves at a quick pace. The Undead Deer will always be chasing down the player and attempting to attack the player if the player isn't looking in it's direction. To fully rid of this entity you must either eat the dead deer corpse fully or destroy the deep corpse with fire and explosions. From there the entity will no longer spawn.
EG Passive Random spawn, spawns once in untitled_87. A dark gray orb about the size of the ATV which emits a deep red glow underneath. Rises very quickly into the sky when encountered.
Soltomia Passive Spawns either at the bottom of the The Hole or can be seen cleaning the player's ATV during the Soltomia Cleaning Event. Soltomia is a Rozital-based entity with 6 flexible, mallaeble, and stretchy metal arms. It moves in a graceful yet wiggly type of way and quite fast, able to outrun the player. It either runs away or hides away from the player.
Shit Duende Passive Spawns during the Shit Duende Event. A Greys-based entity but in a sitting animation and is stationary. The entity slowly fades away when the player encounters it. The head moves around to follow the player.
Black Fogling Passive Will spawn once Black Fog Event. Originally an entity used to trigger the Black Fog Event, now is a black wisp like entity with 2 bright eyes that can be seen even through the darkest areas of the game. It follows the player at a distance and will circle around the player from time to time.

Forbidden Entities

Entity Behavior Spawn Conditions Description Image
Tentacle Balls Neutral Will spawn once Rozital Patrol Event. A tall 6 legged entity with a ball made up of a glassy material and three main eyes, originating from the Rozitals. Its rotating components made up of the black shiny balls. Its eyes light up a purple glow similar to the rozitals. It moves at a running pace, with its steps generating debris and dust particles. When the entity encounters the player for the first time, it will pin the player down and almost study the player for some time before eventually letting go and moving on. If the player attacks or disturbs the entity on its underside, the entity will fire back with a powerful blast that will insta-kill the player. 
The Tentacle Balls are to patrol around the entire map and ensure of no more trouble from the Killer Wisps.
Evil Lambert Aggressive Innaccessible, only spawns in untitled_87. An animated version of the Lambert Plush. It is similar to the Killer Wisp Entity as it will pull apart the player and kill the player, however it glows red and has a red wispy material instead.
Moving Mannequin Neutral After spawning or buying a mannequin you must wait enough time for its pole to be removed. The Moving Mannequin is similar to the Dream Mannquin however it operates a little different, first it starts out as a inanimate mannequin prop that simply moves around the player from time to time while the player isn't looking at it. However, if you were to light it on fire it will begin moving regardless whether or not the player is looking at and begin attacking the player until the player is dead.
Cutout Greys Passive Innaccessible, spawns rapidly in Area 51. The Cutout Greys is exactly the same as the immovable one that you can spawn using the Console Command, but animated. They are hostile to the base or objective in that world. They deal no damage or are hostile to the player, instead hostile to the base. They can spawn with different varieties of themselves including stretched out, long, short, tall, and etc. These can be killed using force, guns, and anything that can break wood.
Tripod Passive Innaccessible, only spawns in untitled_87. The Tripod appears to be passive, non-hostile entity that walks around the map and shakes the player's screen. It has a triangular pyramid for a head and three large animated legs. On its head is a giant purple light beam that shines light that extends for half of the map. The Tripod is extremely tall, wide, and very fast as it is able to even outpace the flying speed of a player's noclip.
Tanneul Passive Spawns anywhere in the world. A type of a tree spirit, is a tall orange and brown figure that can be seen only using a digital or security camera. It can be found wandering around the world randomly. While the entity still exists within the game still wandering around in your world, it cannot be interacted with or by the player as it causes the entity to completely disappear. You also cannot take any pictures of the entity as even looking in its direction will also cause it to disappear. The only way to see this entity is through noclipping in storymode.
Spider Argemia Aggressive Spawns in multiple levels and places: untitled_61, tutorial3 (4 locations) The Spider Argemia is an Argemia doll that has an elongated body, neck, and tail. It also has 8 limbs. The Spider Argemia is only hostile to the player once, after it lunges, chases and presumably attacks the player once, it will leave the player alone. However, some other variations will hurt the player and continue hurting the player until a gameover pops up.
Bean Goombas Passive Innaccessible, only spawns twice in untitled_42. A 2d bean-shaped entity that moves around and if the player touches it forces the player to be teleported back to start. To kill it you need to jump on to it.
Dr Ballsack Aggressive Innaccessible, only spawns in untitled_81. An aggressive ball mesh creature that chases around the player to munch on. It moves at a walking pace and spits out dirt balls of all shapes and sizes every second.
Stalker Plush Passive Innaccessible, only spawns in a horde in untitled_18. The Stalker Plush is a Argemia plushie that follows you behind when you are not looking at it, it is the only entity that can be picked up and be put into the inventory. When you put a Cursed Argemia Plushie into your inventory and drop it, the plushie switches from sitting to standing position, with a gun in its hand it will start jumping towards the player, lunging towards the player, and firing burgers every 1.5 seconds.
Schizophreniac Plush Passive Innaccessible, only spawns twice in untitled_104. Another Argemia plushie that appears in standing form and that is cursed, except it teleports around the player in quick intervals, allowing you to see it for one millisecond.
Dream Mannequin Aggressive Spawns rapidly in the Mannequin Dream. The Dream Mannequin is exactly the same as the normal immovable ones that you find at the main base in Homeworld except animated. The entity will chase down the player until it touches or hits the player. Once it does it results in an incomplete dream for the player.
White Mist Aggressive Spawns once in the Corridor Dream. A white mist that chases down the player until it touches or hits the player resulting in an incomplete dream and sending the player back.
Anomalous Water Aggressive Spawns once in the Fill Dream, Climb Dream, or Jump Dream. The Anomalous Water is an entity that fills the dream room and eventually when fully filling up above the players head it will result in an incomplete dream and sending the player back.
Dream UFO Passive Will spawn overhead in the sky during the UFO Dream. A giant gray UFO that hovers over the player until the dream is ended.
Dream Furfur Passive 4 of them will spawn side by side during the Furfur Dream. Giant version of the Furfur Entity but doesn't disappear and only serves to scare the player.
Duende Aggressive Type alien in the Command Console in VOTV Gray Edition. The earliest version of the Grey, representative of the Greys race within the MrDrNose universe. It is a gray humanoid that chases and repeatedly attacks the player, it can break down doors and is quite strong. It usually spawns in circular swarms. Once killed it will ragdoll like Rufus and Thiccfus.
Gray UFO Passive Replaces the Drone entity and can spawn whenever the player enters the ATV in VOTV Gray Edition. A giant gray UFO that hovers and emits a bright green light below it. It emits a deep bassy sound.
Green Alien Aggressive Replaces the Ariral Entity VOTV Gray Edition. A green humanoid with a UFO diaper and emits warped alien baby noises. It runs significantly faster than an Ariral and can catch up to the player's running speed. It has relatively the same behaviors as the original Ariral entity.
SKELEO Passive Only spawns once at the end of the Halloween Event upon completion. A giant skull shaped entity and a halloween special entity that onces spawned will force open a UI in front of the player that is just a meme. The music comes from The Skull Gang - Kirby Mass Effect.
BS Passive Random spawn, near Stonehenge Landmark, at night. The BS entity is a fast rotating black spherical entity that emits a deep bassy sound. Every 2 seconds a red symbol of whatever shape will glow up on a random side of the entity and it will do this repeatedly.
Cursed Ariral Head Aggressive Innaccessible, entity name is NewBlueprint7_C. Has the head and shape of an Ariral head, however its eyes appear to be gouged out, its mouth carved out, its jaw elongated and stretched out, and the head is relatively large compared to the normal sized Ariral head. This entity moves at a swift pace, appears to fly a little however requires the ground to pathfind. Once spawned the entity will hunt down the player wherever it is at. Once located the player, the entity will latch on to the player almost gobbling it up and cause the game to crash.
Cursed Spider Passive Innaccessible, only appears in untitled_112 and tutorial3. A black 8 limbed entity that teleports around the player in quick intervals, like Shadow man.
Gray Boar Aggressive Innaccessible, only appears in untitled_142. An above-ground hovering cyborg boar with stitches, a badly deformed body and face, and some metallic components attached to it's body. It is another Greys-based entity, however it is one of their many creations. It can outpace the speed of the player's sprint. It can hit player very hard even while the player is no-clipping and it usually takes 2 hits from the entity before it can kill you. When it takes damage or dies it drops a lot of blood onto the ground. It also makes an ATV engine noise as it's chasing the player. The entity also emits a faint yellow light at the bottom.
Poolrooms Entity Passive Spawns only in the basic props interactivity tutorial in tutorial3. An invisible entity that moves things around, makes footsteps that can be heard, opens doors, and can break down wooden boxes. It does this at random and only does it if the player is out of it's view like on a ledge or somewhere else.
Mountain Guys Passive Very rare spawn, usually spawns on the mountain range in OOB (Out of Bounds) area. Three giant figures that can be looked at from a distance.
Chimey Passive Very rare spawn, only during thunderous rain. A chime sound similar to the one heard from the HL2 Interloper ARG will be played (this is not a reference it is only an inspiration), it will spawn within a radius around the player. It appears for a few moments before quickly disappearing. Apparently this entity has a model/visual however it has not been confirmed yet as it is extremely difficult getting a good look at this thing considering the conditions of its spawn.
