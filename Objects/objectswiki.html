Objects

Description:

Like entities, objects exist in the game, however these objects do not move, can be interacted, or are animated in some way. Instead they serve different purposes.

Object Spawn Conditions Description Image
Ship Engine Near Uniform Satellite Dish (X: 637, Y: 44) A orange capsule with a gear shaped housing. Once you trigger the Rozital Ship Event, the purple laser will charge up the machine and once the player gets too close it, it will unlock some of its gears, spin, fly off the rocks, and then proceed to explode in a bright flash.
SCP-432 Near Victor, corner of the map (X: 613, Y: 623) A green metal cabinet that's locked with 9 square shaped keys. Opening it reveals a path to a two-storied room that's full of the same cabinets. Its supposed to represent SCP-432 from the SCP Foundation. It's currently impossible to enter this room by any normal means, even after opening it. Although the teleportation functionality is implemented it requires no-clipping as there is an invisible barrier preventing the player from accessing the room inside.
Basalt Pillars (X: 465, Y: -85) A black basalt pillar that grows out of dirt after you dig it up. They will spread really quick towards the player location if not destroyed.
Invisible Black Rock Near Tango (X: 436; Y: -469) A black rock that can only be seen using a digital camera or security camera. Doesn't do anything but triggering EMF.
Tombstone Near Tango, closer to the river and fence A tombstone with a splat of blood on it. It also triggers and EMF.
Crosses Random Once you find or trigger a Wendigo, a bunch of crosses that appear out of nowhere will spawn near the base. A pair of bones can be found nearby that trigger an EMF.
Maxwell Requires Console Command A cat like object that can be eaten by roaches. It plays music that can be heard from a relatively far radius. It's music has changed several times over the course of updates. In older versions the use command, could be used to toggle his music or not, however in recent versions it causes him to "die", after a short delay his model gets changed to a skeletal version of himself after which the Intro Guitar Riff from Bad To The Bone soon after he explodes spontaneously.
Argemwell Requires Console Command Another cat like object, similar in function to Maxwell however it plays a completely different music and has a different texture. While it's model is the same as Maxwell's, the use command also used to toggle Argem's music on or off but instead causes it to explode with a ton of shrimp that despawn over 5 seconds. The music that is played from this is actually called Eeek! Penis.
Broomba Requires Maxwell and a Roomba item, place both on crafting table and craft A Roomba but overlayed with the Maxwell texture and model. It also plays the Maxwell noises instead of the normal Roomba noises.
Physics Gun Requires a Gravity Gun placed inside a microwave for one minute. A blue version of the gravity gun or overcharged version. It is one of the three variants of the physics gun which is the soft variant. Meaning it isn't very powerful. The object can be used to pick up, carry, and manipulate items. The Physics Gun does have a time limit on it's usage so you can charge up more energy for a longer duration usage with the microwave.
Anperfysium/AB friend Innaccessible. A small purple crystal with the ERROR overlay on it. It is able to move and jump around, there isn't anything that you can interact with, with this object. It makes noises similar to that of Rozitals. Extremely rare object, its spawning location has changed several times over the course of past updates. Its last spawnpoint was at Antibreather Cave at exactly 3:33 am. As of this current version the object is located in a hovering state below the AB cave.
Antibreather Plush A.B. Cave at 3:33 am A plushie that represents a baby version of the Antibreather Entity. Can only be found at the Antibreather Cave at exactly 3:33 am.
Unnamed Rock Stonehenge A dark rock that emits a level 4 EMF signal when near, it is unnamed and doesn't appear to do anything. To obtain you must perform the Hell Opening Event and while the wormhole is going on, make sure to stay as far away as possible and wait 2 minutes until the wormhole disappears and the rock should appear.
Argemia Plush Anywhere A plushie that depicts Argemia, the leader of the Arirals' talls society. It has 3 variations, most can be found across the map. It can be fed shrimp to trigger an Achievement. Once fed with shrimp, the Argemia Plush will increase in size and once 26 shrimp has been fed the Argemia Plush will shrink back to it's original size and pop out an egg.
Nuclear Plush Anywhere A plushie that depicts Argemia except eradiated, the leader of the Arirals' talls society. It has 3 variations, all can be found across the map. It can be fed shrimp to trigger an Achievement. It will eat up both the shrimp and the shrimp package.
Other Argemia Plushes Innaccessible. 6 different variations, each one is unique. None of them can be fed with shrimp, are instead just props. None of them can be found naturally. The white one floats in the air, the black one faces you wherever you are, the gold one is quite heavy, the yellow one has yellow colored eyes, the cyan one has a different face, and the magenta one also has a different face.
Runes Anywhere 4 different variations, the runes are elemental so it'll be easy to distinguish between them: fire, air, water, and earth. These items are required for the Ritual Event in order to obtain the Lambert Plush. To obtain the fire rune you must use a gascan or any explosive and bang a rock on it a few times until it explodes, you could also wait for the Red Wisp to run around and hit a random rock. To obtain the air rune you must fly to the top of the skybox and throw a rock until it touches the very skybox. To obtain the earth rune you must use a metal detector and shovel and dig around the Ariral Treehouse Landmark. To obtain the water rune you must find the fishing gear, craft it, and fish for it in the river.
Rune Slabs Anywhere 4 different variations, each one is used to help you create or find all 4 runes for the Ritual Event. These aren't required as they are meant for extra help if the player cannot figure out how to obtain the runes. Each one can be found at their specified location using the Interactive Map.
Lambert Anywhere A lamb animal plush that has a little healthbar like a boss but doesn't do anything. It heals the player when near. It is a reference to the Cult of the Lamb Game.
Brick Wall Main Base The player can walk in out of this world and the basement like a portal. But be warned when you enter a mysterious force will mess up your base and cause a huge mess to appear. The first part of the structure is very dark and you will hear a growl, then the second part is a very bright staircase with a light brown orangish overlay for each of the textures, then the third part will be the same except with a white overlay, and then finally the last part will have a light blue overlay but will have a barrier stopping you from going deeper. If you drop any items at this point you will lose them to the void. If you ragdoll or return home, you will be taken back to Main Base. Now the deepest part of the staircase is similar to all the other parts listed here except with a dark blue overlay, while currently inaccessible but by all means it is still there.
White Door Random A white door that will spawn randomly in any location, the door can be seen at times to open up into an optical illusion world but not much else.
Rorschach Test Spawns randomly A combination of black inkblots that form a shape on the wall or floor of the base. This is a reference to the real life pyschological experiment performed by Hermann Rorschach.
Thiccfus Plush Kill a Rufus Entity A small plush representing or depicting the Thiccfus Entity, it plays Thiccfus noises when interacted.
